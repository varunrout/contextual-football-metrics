# CxA — Problem Formulation

## 1. Background

Expected Goals (xG) measures the quality of a **shot**. But shots do not arise
from nothing — they are the product of prior creative actions: passes that split
defences, carries that penetrate the final third, cutbacks that drag defenders
out of position. Evaluating only the shot discards the vast majority of attacking
play that cannot be directly attributed to goal probability in traditional
event-based frameworks.

**Contextual Action Value (CxA)** assigns a shot-creation value to each
**creative action** — every pass, carry, cross, and cutback — by estimating
how much expected goal threat that action generates. It answers the question:

> *Given this pass (or carry), how much expected goal quality does it produce
> for the attacking team?*

CxA is therefore the **upstream counterpart** of CxG: CxG quantifies the
value of a shot once it is taken; CxA quantifies the expected goal value
generated by the creative actions that make shots possible.

---

## 2. Formal Problem Statement

### 2.1 Notation

Let $i = 1, \ldots, N$ index creative actions (passes, carries, crosses,
cutbacks) and let $\mathbf{x}_i \in \mathbb{R}^p$ denote the observed feature
vector for action $i$.

Define two binary / continuous outcomes:

| Variable | Type | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| $S_i \in \{0, 1\}$ | binary | 1 if the same possession produces a shot after action $i$ |
| $G_i \in [0, 1]$ | continuous | CxG value of the resulting shot (0 if no shot) |

The **CxA score** for action $i$ is defined as:

$$\boxed{\text{CxA}_i = P(S_i = 1 \mid \mathbf{x}_i) \;\times\; \mathbb{E}\!\left[G_i \mid S_i = 1,\; \mathbf{x}_i\right]}$$

This is a **two-stage expectation**:

- **Stage 1** — Shot-creation probability: the probability that the possession
  containing action $i$ ends in a shot.
- **Stage 2** — Conditional shot quality: the expected CxG of that shot,
  conditioned on a shot actually occurring.

The product is the **marginal expected goal value** generated by action $i$,
marginalised over whether a shot follows.

### 2.2 Why the Product Decomposition?

A naive approach would label an action with the realised CxG of any shot that
followed. This is unbiased in expectation but has high variance — actions that
happen to precede low-probability, high-quality shots are rewarded the same as
those that structurally lead to dangerous situations. The two-stage product
separates:

1. **Does this action tend to create shots at all?** (creation probability)
2. **When shots do follow, how dangerous are they?** (shot quality)

This decomposition also makes the model interrogable: a carry deep in the box
scores highly because both $P(S_i=1)$ is high and $\mathbb{E}[G_i \mid S_i=1]$
is high. A long diagonal switch scores low on both.

---

## 3. Two-Stage Modelling Framework

### 3.1 Stage 1 — Shot-Creation Classifier

Let $p_i = P(S_i = 1 \mid \mathbf{x}_i)$. Stage 1 is a **binary classification
problem**: given the features of creative action $i$, does the same possession
subsequently produce a shot?

$$p_i = f_1(\mathbf{x}_i; \boldsymbol{\theta}_1)$$

where $f_1$ is estimated by minimising binary cross-entropy over all training
creative actions.

Label definition: $S_i = 1$ if any shot event appears in the same possession
(identified by `possession_internal_id`) after action $i$.

Positive rate in training data: **15.93%** (220,899 total actions,
35,168 resulting in a shot).

### 3.2 Stage 2 — Shot-Quality Regressor

Let $q_i = \mathbb{E}[G_i \mid S_i = 1, \mathbf{x}_i]$. Stage 2 is a
**regression problem**: given the features of creative action $i$, what CxG
value does the resulting shot carry?

$$q_i = f_2(\mathbf{x}_i; \boldsymbol{\theta}_2)$$

where $f_2$ is estimated by minimising mean squared error over the **subset of
actions where a shot followed** ($S_i = 1$), using the CxG score of the
resulting shot as the continuous target.

Stage 2 uses the production CxG model (`models/cxg/glm_contextual.joblib`) to
assign CxG scores to shots before attaching labels to training actions.

The full CxA score follows directly:

$$\text{CxA}_i = p_i \;\times\; q_i$$

### 3.3 Scope

Only **creative action types** are scored:

| Action type | Description |
|---|---|
| `pass` | Standard ground/aerial pass |
| `cross` | Delivery from a wide position |
| `carry` | Ball progression on the dribble |
| `cutback` | Pull-back from the byline into the box |

Shots themselves are not scored by CxA (they are scored by CxG). Other
actions (clearances, interceptions, tackles) are outside scope.

## 4. Contextual Conditioning

Traditional action-value models condition primarily on action location — where
on the pitch the action originates. CxA additionally conditions on **opponent
quality**, **match state**, and **receiver context**:

$$\text{CxA}_i^{(\text{cxA})} = P\!\left(S_i = 1 \;\middle|\; \mathbf{l}_i,\;
\underbrace{\mathbf{o}_i}_{\text{opponent}},\;
\underbrace{\mathbf{m}_i}_{\text{match state}},\;
\underbrace{\mathbf{r}_i}_{\text{receiver context}}\right)
\;\times\;
\mathbb{E}\!\left[G_i \;\middle|\; S_i = 1,\; \mathbf{l}_i,\; \mathbf{o}_i,\; \mathbf{m}_i,\; \mathbf{r}_i\right]$$

where $\mathbf{l}_i$ are action location and movement features.

| Context group | Intuition |
|---|---|
| $\mathbf{o}_i$ — Opponent quality | The same pass is more likely to create a shot against a weaker defence |
| $\mathbf{m}_i$ — Match state | Score, minute, and tournament stage shift attacking urgency and defensive shape |
| $\mathbf{r}_i$ — Receiver context | A pass to an unmarked striker in the box is structurally different from a sideways pass in midfield |

---

## 5. Key Modelling Constraints

**C1 — No target leakage.** Features for action $i$ must be computable from
information available at the moment of the action. The shot outcome, CxG value,
and `shot_created` flag must not appear in $\mathbf{x}_i$.

**C2 — Temporal integrity.** Opponent rolling features (e.g.
`opponent_xg_conceded_rolling_5`) must be computed over matches strictly prior
to match $k$.

**C3 — Possession-level label assignment.** Shot-creation labels are assigned
per possession, not per match. A single possession may contain multiple passes
and carries; label $S_i = 1$ is assigned to all creative actions in that
possession if it ends in a shot. This correctly reflects that each action in
a shot-creating possession contributed to that shot opportunity.

**C4 — CxG dependency.** Stage 2 quality labels use CxG scores from the
production CxG model. The CxA training pipeline must be run **after** the
CxG model is trained and saved. Any upgrade to the CxG production model
requires re-training CxA.

## 6. Dataset

### 6.1 Data Sources

| Source | Role | Observations |
|---|---|---|
| WC 2022 + Euro 2020 | Training and internal validation | 220,899 actions |
| Euro 2024 | Held-out test set (never seen during training) | 98,029 actions |

Action types present in the training data: **177,224 passes** and
**141,704 carries** (from the full 318,928-row actions.parquet).

### 6.2 Train/Test Partitioning

Data are partitioned at **competition level**, not row level.

| Role | Competition | Season | Actions | Positive rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `train` | FIFA World Cup + UEFA Euro | 2022 + 2020 | 220,899 | **15.93%** |
| `held_out` | UEFA Euro | 2024 | 98,029 | **19.61%** |

The 19.61% held-out positive rate is higher than the 15.93% training rate.
This likely reflects distribution differences between tournament styles: Euro
2024 may have featured more transitional, direct football with shorter
possession chains and more shots per creative sequence.

### 6.3 Coordinate System

All location features are in the **internal 105×68 metre coordinate frame**
(origin: bottom-left of the own half, x increases toward the opponent goal,
y increases from left to right touchline). StatsBomb raw coordinates are
normalised via:

$$x_{\text{internal}} = x_{\text{SB}} \cdot \frac{105}{120}, \quad
y_{\text{internal}} = y_{\text{SB}} \cdot \frac{68}{80}$$

at ingestion time in `src/ingestion/provider_mapper.py`. All features and
pitch visualisations use the internal 105×68 frame throughout.
