# CxT - Incremental Lift over the Static Zone Baseline

_Added 2026-07-19. Companion to the CxG reconciliation in
`docs/modeling/cxg/07`. Produced by `analysis/22_incremental_lift_cxt.py` on the
Euro 2024 held-out split (competition 55, season 282)._

## 1. What is being tested

CxT is a state-value regression: for an action, predict `possession_cxg`, the
discounted sum of CxG generated by shots later in the same possession
(0.9^k weighting). The honest baseline for a state-value model is the best you
can do knowing only *where* the action happened. So the contextual LightGBM model
is compared against a static zone baseline: the per-zone mean of `possession_cxg`
learned on the training split (16x12 pitch grid) and looked up by zone for the
held-out actions.

This is a fair comparison because the baseline is calibrated to the same target
on the same scale, not an off-the-scale xT surface. Both are scored on the
identical held-out CxT actions (pass, carry, cross, cutback), with a
2,000-sample paired bootstrap on the deltas.

## 2. Result (98,029 held-out actions, target mean 0.0213)

| Predictor | MAE | RMSE | Spearman |
|---|---|---|---|
| static zone baseline | 0.0324 | 0.0713 | 0.180 |
| contextual (LightGBM) | **0.0304** | **0.0697** | **0.303** |

Paired deltas (contextual minus zone baseline), 95% bootstrap CI:

| Metric | Delta | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|
| MAE (lower better) | -0.0020 | [-0.0022, -0.0019] |
| RMSE (lower better) | -0.0016 | [-0.0020, -0.0013] |

**Verdict: the contextual state-value model beats the static zone baseline.**
Both error deltas' CIs exclude zero, and rank correlation improves markedly
(Spearman 0.303 vs 0.180). This is the clearest positive result of the three
metrics: for CxT, context genuinely adds value over just knowing the zone.

## 3. Honest framing

The absolute effect is small (MAE drops ~6%, from 0.0324 to 0.0304) because the
target is heavily zero-inflated (84% of actions generate no shot value), so a lot
of the score is "predict near zero", which the zone baseline also does. The
contextual model's advantage is real and statistically clear, and it is largest
where it matters, on the ranking of high-value states (the Spearman gap). The
comparison is against the project's own honest baseline, not a strawman.

## 4. Does it change a decision? (CONT-08)

`analysis/25_downstream_reranking.py` builds a player threat leaderboard two
ways over the same actions: contextual CxT (the production model) and a static
Karun-Singh xT surface (the value of the zone the action started in). It ranks
676 players by each and looks at the disagreement.

The two rankings agree broadly (rank Spearman 0.88) and the contextual top of the
board is football-sensible (Messi, Alba, Busquets, Pedri, de Jong lead it). The
value is in the movers:

- **Risers under contextual CxT** are deep-lying playmakers and progressive
  ball-players: Joshua Kimmich (+13 in the top 15), Antonio Rudiger (+8), Thiago,
  Jack Grealish, Julian Alvarez. The model credits the build-up context of their
  actions, which the zone surface, blind to context, does not.
- **Fallers** are dominated by goalkeepers and pure defenders (Szczesny, both
  keepers named Martinez, Dubravka, Glik, Lisandro Martinez): high touch volume in
  nominally valuable zones, but low actual threat. The static surface inflates
  them by volume; the contextual model discounts them.

So the metric changes a real decision: if you shortlist threat-creators on the
static xT surface you over-rate high-volume defenders and keepers, whereas
contextual CxT surfaces the deep playmakers instead. That reranking, validated by
the held-out win in section 2, is the point of a contextual metric.
